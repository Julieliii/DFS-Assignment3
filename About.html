    • Nadir Afonso, GOSE (December 4, 1920 - December 11, 2013) was a Portuguese geometric abstractionist painter. After formal architectural training with Le Corbusier and Oscar Niemeyer early in his career, Nadir Afonso studied painting in Paris and became one of the pioneers of kinetic art, working with Victor Vasarely, Fernand Léger, Auguste Herbin and André Block. Herbin and André Brock.

    As a geometric aesthetic theorist in his own right, Nadir Afonso has published several books in which he defends the idea that art is purely objective and governed by the law, that it is not an act of imagination but of observation, perception and formal manipulation.

    Nadir Afonso gained international recognition early in his career, and many of his works are in museums. He is best known for his series depicting cities around the world. He is known to have painted in his later years and died in a hospital in Cascais on December 11, 2013. During his lifetime, he received great honors and represented his country at its best.
